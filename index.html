1. Generic vs Personal Email Performance

Comparison of reply rates between generic company emails (info@, frontdesk@, manager@, etc.) and personal work emails (firstname@company.com). Generic emails make up 12% of all leads contacted.

Key Insight: Generic emails reply at a higher rate (4.5% vs 3.5%) and convert replies to positives at nearly 2x the rate (23.1% vs 12.3%). While personal emails generated more total positives (26 vs 9) due to sheer volume, generic emails are significantly more likely to convert when they do reply — likely because the person managing the inbox is often a decision-maker at smaller businesses.
